Questions, answered.

Is this legal advice?

No. Lexa provides general legal information grounded in sources, and is not a substitute for a licensed attorney. For your specific situation, consult counsel in your jurisdiction.

How does Lexa avoid making things up?

Answers are written only from retrieved source passages, every legal claim must cite one, and an independent verifier checks each claim. If the sources don't support an answer, Lexa says so instead of guessing.

Where does the data come from?

A grounded corpus of public-domain primary law, plus optional real-time results from the U.S. Federal Register, CourtListener case law, and GovInfo — each linked back to its official page.

What do I get on the free plan?

A set number of full research actions per period at no cost. Paid plans raise the limit and let you choose a premium reasoning model.

Can I export or delete my data?

Yes. You can export everything in your account as a file, and delete your account and its data at any time.
